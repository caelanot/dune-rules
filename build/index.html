0.1 Setup
Each faction has a set of unique economic, military, strategic, or treacherous advantages. The object of the game is to use these advantages to gain control of Dune. The winner is the First Player to occupy at least 3 strongholds with at least one of their forces during the Mentat Pause Phase. A player may win alone or in an Alliance with other players.
0.1.1 Prepare
Place all spice tokens in the Spice Bank. Shuffle the Spice Deck and Treachery Deck
0.1.2 Assign Factions
Assign one faction to each player in any way. Determine the seating order randomly. Each player takes their chosen faction's player shield, sheet, and pieces.
0.1.3 Positions
Players place their Player Marker on the player circle closest to their shield and seat at the table.
0.1.4 Traitors
Remove the cards for all factions that are not in play from the Traitor Deck. Shuffle the cards thoroughly and deal each player 4 cards. Each player secretly selects 1 card to keep by placing their chosen card face down behind their Player Shield, returning the other cards face down to the Traitor Deck.
0.1.5 Spice
Spice equal to the amount indicated on each player sheet is removed from the Spice Bank and placed behind each shield.
0.1.6 Forces
Each player's forces are placed on the board as indicated by their player sheet. All forces in reserve are placed next to your shield.
0.1.7 Treachery
Deal 1 card from the Treachery Deck to each player.
0.1.8 Turn Marker
Place the turn marker at 1 on the Turn Track.
0.1.9 First Storm
The Storm Marker is placed at a random location along the map edge using the following procedure - the two players whose Player Markers are nearest on either side of the Storm Start Sector secretly dial a number from 0 to 20 on the Battle Wheels. The two numbers are simultaneously revealed and totaled, and the Storm Marker moved from the Storm Start Sector counterclockwise around the map the sum total of sectors.
1.1 Sequence of Play
DUNE is played over the course of up to 10 turns. Each turn consists of nine specific phases that must be completed in the exact sequence presented below.
1.1.1 Phase 1: Storm
The Storm Marker is moved around the map. The faction whose Player Marker the storm next approaches will be the First Player for this turn.
1.1.1.1 Storm Movement. In all Storm Phases after turn 1, the two players who last used the Battle Wheels will independently dial a number from 1 to 3, simultaneously reveal their numbers, add them together, and then advance the Storm Marker from its current position counterclockwise around the map for the sum total of sectors.
1.1.1.2 Damage. Any forces in a sector of sand territory (except the Imperial Basin) over which the storm passes or stops are killed. Place these forces in the Tleilaxu Tanks. Forces that are not on a sand territory find protection from the storm. In addition, any spice in a sector over which a storm passes or stops is removed to the Spice Bank.
1.1.1.3 Obstruction. Forces may not move into, out of, or through a sector in storm. Forces may not battle if either force is in storm.
1.1.1.4 First Player. The player whose Player Marker the storm next approaches is the First Player for this turn.
1.1.2 Phase 2: Spice Blow and Nexus
The top card of the Spice Deck is turned over and the amount of spice shown on the card is placed in the highlighted territory. If Shai-Hulud appears during the Spice Blow Phase, a Nexus occurs and the players have the opportunity to make and break Alliances.
1.1.2.1 Top Card. The top card of the Spice Deck is turned over.
1.1.2.2 First Turn. During the first turn's Spice Blow Phase only, all sandworm cards turned over are ignored and set aside, then reshuffled back into the Spice Deck after this phase.
1.1.2.3 Territory. This is a Spice Blow. The amount of spice indicated on the card is taken from the Spice Bank and placed onto the territory in the sector containing the Spice Blow icon. Then this card is placed face up on the Spice Deck discard pile. (If the Spice Blow icon is currently in storm, no spice is placed that turn.)
1.1.2.4 Shai-Hulud.
  1. Devour. All spice and forces in the territory shown on the card now face up in the discard pile are removed to the Spice Bank and Tleilaxu Tanks respectively. Then the Shai-Hulud card is placed face up on the Spice Deck discard pile.
  2. Blow. Then another card is turned over. If it is a Shai-Hulud it is immediately discarded and another card is turned over. This continues until a Territory Card appears and spice is placed as defined above. The Territory Card is placed face up on the Spice Deck discard pile.
  3. Nexus. Resolve a (1.1.10) Nexus
1.1.3 Phase 3: CHOAM Charity
Players with 0 or 1 spice may claim CHOAM Charity.
1.1.4 Phase 4: Bidding
Players bid spice to acquire Treachery Cards.
1.1.4.1 Declaration. Before bidding starts, all players must declare how many Treachery Cards they hold.
  1. Hand Limit. The hand limit is 4 cards.
  2. Eligible to Bid. Players at the hand limit are not eligible to bid and must pass during bidding.
1.1.4.2 Dealer. One of the players deals cards from the Treachery Deck face down in a row, 1 card for each player who is eligible to bid.
1.1.4.3 Auction. The first card in the row is now auctioned off for spice.
  1. Starting Bid. The bidding is started by the First Player. If that player is ineligible to bid, the next player to the right who is eligible opens the bidding.
  2. Bidding. The player who bids first must bid 1 or more spice or pass. Bidding then proceeds to the bidder's immediate right. The next bidder may raise the bid or pass and so on around the table until a top bid is made and all other players pass. The top bidding player then pays the number of spice they bid into the Spice Bank and takes the card.
1.1.4.4 Bid Limit. Players may not bid more spice than they have.
1.1.4.5 Next Starting Bidder. In subsequent bidding during this phase, the first eligible player to the right of the player who opened the bid for the previous card begins the bidding for the next card.
1.1.4.6 End of Bidding.
  1. Full Auction. Bidding for Treachery Cards continues until all cards available for bid have been auctioned off.
  2. Unwanted. If a card is passed on by everyone, all remaining cards are returned in order to the top of the Treachery Deck and the Bidding Phase is over.
1.1.4.7 Transparency. The number of Treachery Cards a player has in their hand must be revealed upon request by another player during the Bidding Phase.
1.1.4.8 Time Limit. Each player must bid within 10 seconds of the previous player, or they are assumed to have passed.
1.1.5 Phase 5: Revival
All players are allowed to reclaim forces and leaders from the Tleilaxu Tanks.
1.1.5.1 Force Revival. All players may now revive up to 3 forces from the Tleilaxu Tanks.
  1. Free. A certain number of forces are revived for free as stated on the player sheet.
  2. Spice. Any additional forces that may be revived must be done at a cost of 2 spice per force.
  3. To Reserves. Revived forces must be placed in the player's reserves.
1.1.5.2 Leader Revival. When a player has no Active Leaders, they may revive 1 face up leader per turn.
  1. Fighting Strength. To revive a leader, a player must pay that leader's fighting strength in spice.
  2. Revived Leader Status. A revived leader can be played normally and is still subject to being a traitor.
  3. Dead Again. If a revived leader is killed again, place it face down in the Tleilaxu Tanks. This leader cannot be revived until all of the player's other revivable leaders have been revived, killed, and sent to the Tleilaxu Tanks again.
1.1.5.3 Spice Bank. All spice paid for revival is placed in the Spice Bank.
1.1.6 Phase 6: Shipment and Movement
Starting with the First Player and proceeding counterclockwise, each player in turn ships forces down to the planet or brings in forces from the Southern Hemisphere (Fremen) and then moves their forces on the game board.
1.1.6.1 Force Shipment.
  1. Shipment of Reserves. A player with off-planet reserves may make one shipment of any number of forces from their reserves to any one territory on the map.
  2. Payment. A player must pay spice to the Spice Bank.
  3. Price. The cost of shipping off-planet reserves is 1 spice per force shipped into any stronghold, and 2 spice per force shipped into any non-stronghold territory.
  4. Sectors. When shipping into a territory lying in several sectors, a player must make clear in which sector of the territory they choose to leave their forces.
  5. Exceptions.
    1. Storm. No player may ship into or out of a sector in Storm.
    2. Occupancy Limit. No player may ship into a stronghold already occupied by two other players.
    3. One Way. No player may ship forces from the board back to their reserves.
1.1.6.2 Force Movement. Each player may move, as a group, any number of their forces from one territory into one other territory. Unless separated by Storm, that player may move groups of forces from different sectors of the same territory.
  1. One Force Move. Each player may make only one force move per turn.
  2. One Adjacent Territory. A player without a force in either Arrakeen or Carthag at the start of their move can only move their forces to one adjacent territory.
  3. Ornithopters. A player who starts a force move with one or more forces in either Arrakeen, Carthag, or both has access to Ornithopters and may move forces up to three adjacent territories. The forces moved do not have to be in Arrakeen or Carthag to make the move.
  4. Sectors. Sectors have no effect on movement. Forces can move into or through a territory ignoring all sectors.
  5. Storm. As defined in the (1.1.1.3) Storm Phase section, no force may move into, out of, or through a sector in storm. Many territories occupy several sectors, so that a player may move into and out of a territory that is partly in the storm, so long as the group does not pass through the part covered by the storm.
    1. Sectors. When ending a move in a territory lying in several sectors, a player must make clear in which sector of the territory they choose to leave their forces.
    2. Polar Sink. The Polar Sink is never in storm.
  6. Occupancy Limit. Like shipment, forces cannot be moved into or through a stronghold if forces of two other players are already there.

1.1.8 Phase 8: Spice Collection
Forces in territories that contain spice may collect the spice.
1.1.9 Phase 9: Mentat Pause Phase
Factions either declare a winner (or winners), or take some time to evaluate their positions on the map and then move the Turn Counter to the next position on the Turn Track to begin the next turn.
1.1.9.1 Solo Stronghold Victory. When one player controls 3 strongholds with at least one of their Forces during the Mentat Pause Phase, that player wins the game.
1.1.9.2 Allied Stronghold Victory. When two players in an Alliance separately control a total of at least 4 strongholds with one or more Forces during the Mentat Pause Phase, that Alliance wins the game.
1.1.9.3 Multiple Victories. When multiple players or alliances achieve a stronghold victory, only the player or alliance first in Storm Order wins.
1.1.9.4 War Continues. When there are no winners, players mull over their positions on the board, consider their options and, when they are ready, move the turn marker to the next position on the Turn Track to begin the next turn.
1.1.9.5 Game End. After the last turn, the game is over and a winner is declared.
  1. Fremen. If Spacing Guild are not in the game, the Fremen win.
  2. Spacing Guild. If Spacing Guild is in the game, the Spacing Guild wins.
  3. Stronghold Victory. The players with the most strongholds win.

2.1 Atreides
You have limited prescience.
2.1.1 Setup
2.1.1.1 Forces. 10 forces in Arrakeen and 10 forces in reserves.
2.1.1.2 Spice. Start with 10 spice.
2.1.1.3 Free Revival. 2 forces.
2.1.2 Advantages
2.1.2.1 Filament Book. You, and only you, may keep written notes about Treachery Cards.
2.1.2.2 Bidding. During the bidding round, you may look at each Treachery Card as it comes up for purchase before any faction bids on it
2.1.2.3 Wormsign. During (1.1.6) Shipment and Movement Phase, before anyone ships, you may look at the top card of the Spice Deck.
2.1.2.4 Prescience. During the Battle Phase, you may force your opponent to reveal your choice of one of the four elements they will use in their Battle Plan against you: the leader, the weapon, the defense, or the number dialed. If you choose to ask about a weapon or defense and your opponent tells you that they are not playing that element during this battle, you may not then ask to see a different element.
2.1.3 Alliance
2.1.3.1 Prescience. You may assist your allies by forcing their opponent to show them one element of their Battle Plan.
2.1.4 Advanced Game Advantages
2.1.4.1 Special Karama Power. You may use a Karama Card to look at any one player's entire Battle Plan.
2.1.4.2 Kwisatz Haderach. The Kwisatz Haderach starts out inactive and the Kwisatz Haderach token may not be used. Use the Kwisatz Haderach card and counter token to secretly keep track of forces lost in battle. Once you have lost 7 or more forces in battles, the Kwisatz Haderach token becomes active for the rest of the game.
  1. Kwisatz Haderach. In your battle, you may add the Kwisatz Haderach token to your leader or Cheap Hero's strength in one territory per turn.
    1. Unprotected. If the leader or Cheap Hero is killed, the Kwisatz Haderach does not add its +2 strength.
  2. Atreides Loyalty. A leader accompanied by Kwisatz Haderach cannot turn traitor.
  3. Prophecy Blinded. The Kwisatz Haderach can only be killed if blown up by a lasgun/shield explosion.
  4. Reawaken. When killed, the Kwisatz Haderach token must be revived like any other leader.
  5. Ascension. Alive or dead, the Kwisatz Haderach does not prevent the Atreides from reviving other leaders.
2.2 Bene Gesserit
You are adept in the ways of mind control.
2.2.1 Setup
2.2.1.1 Forces. 1 force in Polar Sink and 19 forces in reserves.
2.2.1.2 Spice. Start with 5 spice.
2.2.1.3 Free Revival. 1 force.
2.2.2 Advantages
2.2.2.1 Prediction. After (0.1.3) Positions, secretly predict when a faction will win, placing a turn number card and a faction card face down behind your Player Shield. If the faction you predicted wins (alone or as an ally, even your ally) in the turn you predicted, reveal your prediction and win alone.
  1. Special Victory. You cannot predict the Spacing Guild or Fremen will win with their Special Victory conditions.
2.2.2.2 Spiritual Advisors. Whenever any other faction ships forces onto Dune from off-planet, you may ship 1 force for free from your reserves into the Polar Sink.
2.2.2.3 Voice. You may command your opponent to play or not play one of the following cards in their Battle Plan:
  • poison weapon
  • projectile weapon
  • poison defense
  • projectile defense
  • a worthless card
  • a Cheap Hero
  • a specific special weapon by name
  • or a specific special defense by name.
Your opponent must comply with your command as well as they are able to.
2.2.3 Alliance
2.2.3.1 Voice. You may Voice your ally's opponent in battle.
2.2.4 Advanced Advantages
Your forces have two sides, the spiritual, striped side (advisor), and the battle side with no stripes (fighter). Fighters are normal forces.
2.2.4.1 Charity. You always receive CHOAM charity of 2 spice, regardless of how much spice you already have.
2.2.4.2 Karama. You may use any worthless card as if it was a Karama card.
2.2.4.3 Advisors.
  1. Advising. Whenever any other faction ships forces to Dune from off-planet, you may instead ship one advisor for free from your reserves into that same territory (instead of the Polar Sink). You may only do this when you do not have fighters already present in that territory.
  2. Coexistence. Advisors coexist peacefully with other faction forces in the same territory. Advisors have no effect on the play of the other factions whatsoever and cannot collect spice, be involved in combat, prevent another faction's control of a stronghold, prevent another faction from challenging a stronghold (Occupancy Limit), use ornithopters, or play Family Atomics. Advisors are still susceptible to storms, sandworms, lasgun/shield explosions, and Family Atomics.
2.2.4.4 Fighters.
  1. Enlistment. When you move advisors into an occupied territory, they may remain as advisors or flip to fighters.
  2. Battle Stance. When advisors are ever alone in a territory before (1.1.7) Battle Phase, they automatically flip to fighters.
  3. Unison. When you move advisors or fighters into a territory where you have the opposite type, they flip to match the type already in the territory.
  4. Ally. Advisors cannot flip to fighters with an ally present.
  5. Storm. Advisors cannot flip to fighters under storm.
2.2.4.5 Intrusion. When another faction ships or moves into a territory where you have fighters, you may flip them to advisors.
2.2.4.6 Battle. During (1.1.6) Shipment and Movement Phase, before anyone ships, in each territory that you have advisors, you may flip all of those advisors to fighters.

2.4 Fremen
You are native to Dune and know its ways.
2.4.1 Setup
2.4.1.1 Forces. 10 forces distributed as you like on Sietch Tabr, False Wall South, and False Wall West; and 10 forces in reserves (on the far side of Dune).
2.4.1.2 Spice. Start with 3 spice.
2.4.1.3 Free Revival. 3 forces (you cannot buy additional revivals)
2.4.2 Advantages
2.4.2.1 Shipment. During shipment, you may bring any or all of your reserves for free onto the Great Flat or onto any one territory within two territories of the Great Flat (subject to storm and occupancy rules)
2.4.2.2 Movement. During movement, you may move your forces two territories instead of one.
2.4.2.3 Shai-Hulud.
  1. Devour. If Shai-Hulud appears in a territory where you have forces, they are not devoured.
  2. Ride the Worm. Upon conclusion of the Nexus, you may ride the sandworm and move some or all of the forces in the territory to any territory subject to storm and occupancy rules.
    1. Ending Territory. Any forces in the target territory are not devoured.
    2. Multiple Worms. If Shai-Hulud appears again and you still have forces in the original territory, you may do this again.
2.4.2.4 Fremen Special Victory Condition. If no faction has won by the end of the last turn, your plans to alter Dune have succeeded, and you and any allies win the game if you meet the following criteria.
  1. Sietch Tabr and Habbanya Sietch. You or no one occupies Sietch Tabr and Habbanya Sietch.
  2. Tuek's Sietch. Neither Harkonnen, Atreides, Emperor, nor Richese occupies Tuek's Sietch.
2.4.3 Alliance
2.4.3.1 Shai-Hulud. You may protect your ally from being devoured by sandworms.
2.4.3.2 Free Revival. You may raise your ally's free revival to 3.
2.4.3.3 Fremen Special Victory Condition. Your ally wins with you when you win with the (2.4.2.4) Fremen Special Victory Condition.
2.4.4 Advanced Advantages
2.4.4.1 Special Karama Power. During the (1.1.2) Spice Blow Phase, use a Karama Card to place a sandworm in any sand territory that you wish. This is treated as a normal sandworm and causes a Nexus.
2.4.4.2 Storm Rule.
  1. Storm. Shuffle the Storm Deck, and randomly select a card and place it next to the board. When the Storm is moved, move the Storm the amount of spaces indicated on the card.
  2. Forecast. You may look at the next Storm Card.
2.4.4.3 Sandworms. During a Spice Blow, all additional sandworms that appear after the first sandworm may be placed by you in any sand territory you wish.
2.4.4.4 Storm Losses.
  1. Half Loss. If your forces are caught in a storm, only half of them are killed (any fractions are rounded up).
  2. Ship into Storm. You may bring your reserves into storm at half loss.
2.4.4.5 Fedaykin. Your three starred forces, Fedaykin, have a special fighting capability.
  1. Fedaykin Strength. Fedaykin forces are worth two normal forces in battle.
  2. Fedaykin Revival. Only one Fedaykin force can be revived per turn.
2.4.4.6 Battles. Your forces do not require spice to count at their full strength.
